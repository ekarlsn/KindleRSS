Apr. 14, 2027 · The Stargazers Weekly Editorial Team

The Perennids meteor shower reaches its annual peak this weekend, and forecasts are calling for exceptionally dark skies across most of the northern hemisphere. This year's display is expected to produce up to 90 meteors per hour at peak — one of the stronger showings in the past decade.

Peak activity is centred on the night of Saturday the 16th into Sunday morning. The radiant rises well clear of the horizon by local midnight, so the best window for most observers will be: 

01:15 – 04:30  local time  (radiant highest, limiting magnitude ~6.8)

You don't need any special equipment — the naked eye is the right tool for meteor watching. That said, a reclining chair and a wide field of view go a long way.

What to bring

Comfort matters more than optics on a meteor night. Pack for a long stay and you will enjoy it far more than someone who dashes out for ten minutes.

  • A reclining garden chair or sleeping mat — you will be looking straight up for an extended period
  • Warm layers even in mild weather; lying still in the small hours gets cold quickly
  • A red-light torch to preserve your dark adaptation while still being able to find things
  • A notepad or the Stargazers Weekly logging app if you want to contribute to the citizen-science count

Things to actively avoid:

  • Phone screens at full brightness — each glance costs you roughly 20 minutes of dark adaptation. Enable night mode and turn brightness to minimum before you head out
  • Light-polluted sites — even a modest drive away from town can double the number of meteors you see; our dark-sky map shows good spots near most major cities
  • Binoculars or a telescope — the field of view is far too narrow; you will miss almost everything
